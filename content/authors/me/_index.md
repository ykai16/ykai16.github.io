I am an **AI × Omics** researcher at the frontier of drug discovery and computational biology. As a Principal Scientist at **Bristol Myers Squibb (BMS)**, I lead efforts that integrate large-scale **genomics**, **multi-omics**, and artificial intelligence to accelerate the development of next-generation therapeutics.

My research is organized around three interconnected themes:

**Targeted Protein Degradation & CELMoD Biology.** I apply genome-wide epigenomic and transcriptomic profiling to dissect the mechanisms of action of **CELMoD** agents—cereblon E3 ligase modulators that selectively degrade transcription factors previously considered undruggable. My work has contributed to the mechanistic understanding of **Golcadomide** and **Mezigdomide**, two clinical-stage CELMoD agents that degrade **IKZF1 (Ikaros)** and **IKZF3 (Aiolos)** in hematologic malignancies. More recently, I have investigated **ZBTB7A/WIZ dual degradation** by **BMS-986470**, a novel degrader that potently reactivates fetal hemoglobin (**HbF**) for the treatment of **sickle cell disease (SCD)** and **thalassemia**.

**Hemoglobinopathy Biology & HbF Reactivation.** Pharmacologic reactivation of HbF is a clinically validated strategy to mitigate the severity of SCD and β-thalassemia. Using CRISPR screens, single-cell multi-omics, and high-resolution chromatin architecture mapping (Micro-C), I work to define the transcriptional and epigenomic mechanisms that govern the fetal-to-adult globin switch—and how targeted protein degradation of key repressors like **ZBTB7A** and **WIZ** can reverse it.

**AI Agents & Multi-omics for Drug Discovery.** I develop and deploy **AI agents** and machine learning pipelines on large-scale biological datasets spanning **genomics**, transcriptomics, proteomics, and CRISPR screening. These tools address core drug discovery challenges: target identification, patient stratification, biomarker discovery, and the rational design of degraders and molecular glues. I am particularly interested in how **large language models (LLMs)** and autonomous AI agents can compress the timeline from biological insight to clinical candidate.

Prior to BMS, I was a postdoctoral fellow at **Harvard Medical School** and the **Dana-Farber Cancer Institute**, where I studied chromatin regulation and super-enhancer biology under Stuart Orkin and Guo-Cheng Yuan. I completed my Ph.D. in Computational Biology at **George Washington University**.
